You can listen to episodes of "Off The Hook" by clicking on the "play" button on the RealAudio console on each page. The realaudio files have been embedded so you must wait for them to be downloaded prior to playing them.

Synopsis: 1994 - 1995



Phiber Optik went to prison in this series of shows and many of the listeners of "Off The Hook" felt as if they went with him. During the ten months of his absence, communications gradually improved - from rumors to recordings and, finally, to live telephone calls that weren't supposed to be possible. In November, Phiber returned to a hero's welcome and a "parade" down Broadway. 1994 also saw the infamous Hackers On Planet Earth (HOPE) conference in August. The darkness returned in 1995, though. That's when Bernie S. began to go through hell with the Secret Service and it's all captured here.

Also: updates on the Clipper Chip, the first signs that Kevin Mitnick is being pursued and the first reports of his capture, New York City's Metrocard is introduced as is the minidisc, the Scientologists initiate raids against Internet providers and anonymous remailers, and the Quest for ISDN begins. The first reviews of "The Net" and "Hackers" also appear here.

Interviews with Mike Godwin, Lyn McNulty, Acid Phreak, Lee Nessbaum, Robert Steele, Joe Stevala, Rop from Hacktic, Brock Meeks, Bernie S., John Markoff, Dave Banisar, Thomas Icom, Roman Kazan, Billsf, Razor, Andy from the Chaos Computer Club, Steve O'Keefe, Daniel Forrest, Dave Harrison, Xenophile, Pam and Jim from Esoteric Resources, Nick Barnes, Glen McGuiness, Master Chemist, Joker, and Eric Hughes.
