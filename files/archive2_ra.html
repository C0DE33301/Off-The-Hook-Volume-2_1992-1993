Synopsis: 1992 - 1993



This is when "Off The Hook" really started to turn into the voice of the hacker community and the timing couldn't have been better. Ominous developments were all around in the early part of 1992. Phiber Optik became a regular part of the show and shared insight into the raids that affected him and other hackers. Other historical milestones included the raid of a California BBS operator who would later turn into a martyr for the cause of free speech, as well as a changing of the tide in Holland. The Michelangelo virus was a big hit with the media but not many others.

Topics include Digital Telephony, the Clipper Chip, the Rodney King verdict, the debut of Sneakers, the Secret Service raid of a 2600 meeting, the lawsuit against the Secret Service by Steve Jackson, and the World Trade Center explosion. Caller ID and whitehouse.gov made their debuts.

1993 ended on a sour note as Phiber Optik was sentenced to a year in prison. Interviews with Rop from Holland, George Gleason, Robert Thomas, Craig Neidorf, Fred Cohen, Mark Ludwig, Mike Godwin, Don Delaney, John Draper, Billsf, Peter Barnes, Bruce Sterling, Bernie S., Brock Meeks, Inhuman, John Perry Barlow, Julian Dibbell, Steve Jackson, Mr. French, Jim Thomas, Glen Roberts, Phil Zimmerman, James Henderson, Marc Rotenberg, Bill from RNOC, Razor, and Robert Steele.
